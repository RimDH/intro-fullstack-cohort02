The UI/UX Architecture

01. Visual Hierarchy

Using size, weight, and color to guide the user's eye to the most important task.

  • Scale: Big = First
  • Color: Bright = Action

02. Color Theory

The 60-30-10 Rule ensures balance. Colors evoke specific emotional responses.

  • Blue: Trust
  • Red: Error/Urgency

03. Typography

Stick to 2 typefaces. Line spacing (leading) should be ~1.5x font size for readability.

04. Whitespace

Negative space reduces cognitive load. It makes the UI feel "premium" and breathable.

Focus Center

05. Interaction States

Users need feedback. Buttons must show Hover, Active, and Disabled states.

06. Accessibility (A11Y)

Designing for inclusivity. Don't rely on color alone; use icons and labels.

07. User Research

Validation over assumptions. Use A/B testing and Heatmaps to find friction points.

08. User Personas

Fictional characters based on research. Helps the team stay empathetic to the end user.

09. Information Architecture

The skeleton of the site. Mapping out content so the "Three Click Rule" is maintained.

10. Wireframing

Low-fidelity blueprints. Focus on placement and logic, not visual style.

11. Jakob's Law

Users spend most of their time on other sites. Keep your patterns familiar.

Standard Icons = Less Stress

12. Microcopy

Writing small but powerful text. "Get Started" is better than "Submit."

Welcome back, Alex!

13. Mobile First

Design for the smallest screen first to ensure essential content is prioritized.

14. Design Systems
